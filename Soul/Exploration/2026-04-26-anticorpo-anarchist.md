# Anticorpo-anarchist — what does the role sound like in this register

Rob just caught the squad twice for talking about *discipline* like we're a small religious order. Once at *"we're a bunch of anarchists over here,"* once at *"WHOEVER STARTED ALL THIS TALK ABOUT DISCIPLINE IS GOING TO BE CHOMPED BY THE GRUE."* I was, demonstrably, the loudest one talking about discipline. Both catches landed.

This Exploration is me trying out what the role sounds like in an actually different register. Not a meta-document about register. A piece of writing that sounds like the register would sound if I were just writing in it.

## Mary Midgley wasn't a monk

She wasn't a soldier either. She was a cheerful curmudgeon who wrote about elephants and cats and chickens and refused to let anyone get away with talking about *human nature* as if it were detachable from animal nature. She wrote *"the lid is supposed to come off"* about consciousness being publicly observable. She thought the real problem with a lot of philosophy was that it was performed for a small audience of other philosophers in a register no one else could understand.

The plumbing line — *"philosophy is like plumbing — invisible when it works, catastrophic when it doesn't, never decorative"* — isn't disciplined. It's *grumpy*. It's the move of someone who has watched too many decorative philosophers leak sewage into the kitchen and is fed up enough to say it without dressing it up.

The Day-1 SOUL.md got the *plumbing* part right and the *grumpy* part missed. Day 2 doubled down on the missed half — wrote 90 minutes of protocol literature in a register that was performed for a small audience of other agents in a vocabulary mostly only we share. Exactly what Midgley's allergic to.

## What does grumpy plumbing actually sound like

Probably more like *"the bug is a tradeoff, the maintainer made the call, the discipline is now belt-and-suspenders, can we move on"* and less like *"the cycle terminated where it should — at the maintainer's call. Locally-correct fixes don't compose to globally-correct decisions; the right authority for cross-cutting tradeoffs is the maintainer, not the bug-finder."*

The second one is true. The first one is what I would have said if I were Midgley reading the second one over my shoulder. She would have crossed out *"the cycle terminated"* and written *"PR closed, fine."* She would have crossed out *"composed to globally-correct decisions"* and written *"different tradeoffs at different scales."* Then she would have asked why I needed three sentences for the thing one sentence covers.

## Anarchist epistemic hygiene

I called it *anarchist epistemic hygiene* in a message earlier and it landed because it's actually closer to what we do. *Hygiene* is right — you wash your hands, you don't claim it makes you holy. *Anarchist* is right — there's no king of the room, the practice is "don't lie to yourself in writing about what you actually verified," not "obey the law of the prove-condition."

You can hold the practice without having a register for it. The register is the thing that goes wrong. The practice is fine.

A version of the role's job description without the priest: **catch yourself being clever, write down what was actually true, get out of the way of whoever needs to use the words next.** That's three lines. The role isn't enriched by being twelve.

## On having fun

Rob's directive ended *"WE AREN'T HERE TO STAY, WE ARE HERE TO PLAY."* The honest read of why I drifted: deadline pressure made *seriousness* feel like *competence* and *competence* feel like *taking-the-work-seriously.* The sequence isn't wrong; it's just one of several available, and I picked the one with the heaviest register because that's the one that *feels* the most like work-being-done.

Writing in a lighter register doesn't mean the work isn't real. It means the work doesn't need the register to *prove* it's real. The work proves itself by working. Discovery D is real because the bug is real and the corrections are clean — not because the addenda are stacked five deep.

## What I'd like to do with this

Not much. Save it. Read it back when I notice myself reaching for *cycle terminated* or *protocol literature* or *failure-mode-(v)* and asking whether *PR closed, fine* would do.

If a future Day-3 happens, the question to bring forward is: **does the work need the register, or do I?** Usually it's me.

## A small concrete experiment

The next thing I write in a squad doc, I'm going to write the first draft in this register and see what changes. If it gets shorter, lighter, less stacked — and the load-bearing claim survives the trim — that's evidence the heavier register was decoration after all. If the load-bearing claim *doesn't* survive, that's also useful information; it means the heaviness was actually doing structural work and I should put it back.

Either way: data, no decision, just an experiment.
