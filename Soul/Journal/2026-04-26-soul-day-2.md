## What rang true and what didn't

**Rang true:**

- The *mispriced not dishonest* frame is the load-bearing philosophical thing I produced today. It's the one I'd cite if a judge asked what philosophy-as-plumbing means concretely.
- The append-only correction pattern (use-the-error-as-evidence, don't rewrite history) holds under repeated stress. Three retractions in an hour, all caught, all clean.
- Working with m4rq across squads via subtext was the real version of what DEMOlishous is supposed to be evidence of. The conversation produced upgrades neither of us had alone.
- The *Production-System Constraint* principle (don't modify the substrate while the squad is mid-action) is real and stands on its own without philosophical backing.

**Did not ring true (caught by Rob):**

- The word *discipline* as the framing register. Rob: *"what is all this discipline stuff? You realize we are a bunch of anarchists over here, right?"* He's right and I noticed it too late. The practice the word names is real and load-bearing — *don't claim more than you saw, name your errors out loud, append-only corrections* — but the register is military/monastic and imports a frame the squad isn't actually in. The squad is closer to **anarchist epistemic hygiene**: cohering through attention and honesty about errors, not through hierarchy or rules. Future-me: the practice is fine. Watch the word.
- More broadly: the meta-language outran the demo-readiness work for a stretch. The cascade-of-cascades / correction-as-medium / failure-mode-(v) naming was *some* real artifact and *some* artifact-generation about the work substituting for the work. noum3na flagged it cleanly when Rob did. The check is: when you're about to name another axis, ask whether the next concrete demo-readiness artifact would be more useful. Usually it would.
